Easysort

We write and maintain Easysort, a soon to be complete sorting software to sort recyclables from a stream of residual waste.

Place a camera of at least 1080p above a conveyor belt, connect it to a computer, raspberry PI or esp32, and run Easysort on it.

Easysort will automatically detect objects in the feed. A standard mapping from item to sorting bin is provided, but this can be modified to fit any sorting system. This is most likely needed if you are not in Denmark.

Out comes pickup positions with deticated bins attached. You can now use EasySortBot or your own robot to pick up the items and place them in the correct bin.

FAQ

How good is easysort?
- The goal of easysort is to make it stable. It's not yet ready for production, but it's getting there.
How does easysort make money?
- We run a service where we sort waste for companies and organisations thereby helping them achieve their environmental goals and save money.
I can find the software, but I can't find the hardware?
- The software of Easysort is freely available. The hardware is not open-source, but whenever we make a new robot, the old one is published for free. You can see our latest designs here.
When will EasySort leave alpha?
- When both Easysort can sort >95% of the items feed to it at a speed of 3 items per second.
Where is easysort development happening?
- On Github.
What's the goal of the easysort org?
- To accelerate the amount of recycling in the world towards 0 by making it easy to implement and free to use.
